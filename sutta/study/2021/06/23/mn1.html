MN1 - Mūlapariyāya Sutta - Root of all things

Notes on videos

Mūla means root - can also be root cause, or the first or beginning Kūsala Mūla - wholesome roots Pariyati - The learning of the scriptures / Also means in suttas to a discourse Pari - means around

Sabbdhammamūla pariyāya - Phenomena

Puthujjana - common person / ordinary / (multitude of them, distinct from the noble ones) / wordling

The Puthujjana (the uninstructed worlding)

The Puthujjana sees earth as earth

  • Perceives earth as earth (with a distortion occuring)

A subtle distortion is entering into the perception of earth. Due to deep diflements which infiltrate into the act of perception. Disposition towards a distortion/misconception

Commentary: 4 distortions of perceptions

  • What is Impermenant to be permanent
  • Dhukka to be a source of happiness
  • What is non-self to be self
  • What is impure to be pure

Grasping upon the appearance of the object as concrete and substantial

  • Having perceived earth as earth, he conceives(Maññati) earth, he conceives in the earth, he conceives from earth, he conceives earth to be mine

Misconceives possibly more accurate than conceives

Maññati - conceives - distorted thinking, thinking that is influenced by the mental defilements From root Man to think. Not simple discursive thinking but developed thinking, one that is unwholesole. Building upon the distorted perception to apprehend the object in a mode contrary to its actual nature.

Maññana is the noun i think?

Modes of misconceiving / thought influenced by 3 defilements (Āsava? or Kilesa? Āsava are Kilesa, Kilesa is the general broadterm?): Craving , Conceit and Wrong view

- Through Craving - one is inclined/taking to me mine
- Through Conceit - one develops/gives rise to the idea "I am" / "I act".
- Grasping on I, therefore "I am", "what is this I?" -> the wrongview of self

Thinking of earth with [Craving , Conceit and Wrong view]

  1. Directly conceives earth (conceives to be identical, as self)
  2. Locative, he conceives in the earth, as existing in the earth (conceives self to exist within earth (body made of 4 elements))
  3. Ablative case, apart from, seperate from the earth (taking self to be self identity seperate to the material elements)
  4. He conceives earth to be mine (taking the self to possess the form)

He delights in earth.

After misconceving/miscontruing it (i.e taking to be mine), then the worlding enjoys/takes delight (motivated by craving or conceit) in the earth element.

Why does this happen? - Because he has not fully understood it.

apariññāta - not fully known/understood

What is meant by fully understanding something? - 3 kinds of full understanding: The task in the development of insight Vipassana isn’t just being mindful of the present Its about insight into the nature of phenomena 3 stages of deepening insight 1. Full understanding of the known. One investigates with concentration mind the nature of phenomema to determine what they are: “this is form”, “this is feeling”, “this is consciousness” - Defining the aggregtates, the nanarupa in terms of distinct characteristics (Hardness solidity). What are the conditions for the arising of these aggregates.

            2. Full understanding through examination in terms of the 3 characteristics. After seeing what it is, one perceives them in terms of 3 characteristics.

            3. Full understanding through abandoning. As insight becomes deeper and sharper, one starts to abandon distorted perceptions and views. As they do this they abandon craving and attachment to the conditioned phenomena.

i.e 1. not seeing earth as material form. solidity, 2. not seen earth as non-self, impermanent, non self 3. hasn’t discarded wrong views about the earth

Then goes through 24 different things (bases of conceiving), x as x… , earth, fire, water, air. beings, gods, devas, 4 levels of formless meditive attainments - The base infinite space - The base of infinite consciousness - The base of nothingness - The base of neither perception nor non perception

(These are the ones the initial teachers of the buddha mastered)
,
The seen (visible form, eye consciousness), the heard (ear consciousness), the sensed (the sensed - everything that comes within the range of the other senses, smell, taste, touch) and the cognised (everything thought, conceived, imagined, reflected, ideas, decisions)
, 
Unity/oneness (ekkata?) (states of meditation absorption), diversity/difference (nanata?) (totallity of non meditative experience)
,
all as all (the whole universe of conditioned phenomena),
,
nibbanna

Note: Kalyāna-Puthujjana - is a virtious worlding engaging in the training/gained some knowledge of the dhamma and is engaged in sincere practice

Learner or trainee - Sekha (say-ka) - one who has reached stream entry

Also has a meaning of anyone engaged in the training
    But usually means someone who is a stream-enterer up to Arahant
    [ Bhikkhu engaged in higher training ]

    "directly knows earth as earth". (abhijānāti - superior knowing (jānāti - to know)), knowing clearly, knowing in depth, knowing accurately, and suggests directness

    Knowing it, in the framework of the 4 noble truths. i.e the 3 characteristics. Knowing the origin. knowing the cessation, knowing the way to its cessation.

    Doesn't mention the perception distortion. (because he has right view?)
    
    "He should not conceive" - (Mā Maññi) (Mā is a prohibition, "should not" like yaa in thai)

    "He should not conceive because he must/should fully understand it"

    pariññeyyam - "shoud fully understand it" - comes from the verb - parijānāti - fully know

    So the Sekha has abhijānāti (direct knowledge), but not yet parijānāti(full complete understanding). The purpose of training is to advance from abhijānāti to parijānāti. 
    To advance the disciple is given the words: Do not misconceive what you are perceiving.

    The disciple in higher training has eradicated "Wrong views of self". Even the disciple in training still has craving and conceit. So the disciple can still engage in misconceiving in terms of craving and concept - hence, the buddha saying "Do not misconceive" (but isn't about the 3rd - wrong views of self, as he's abandoned this due to attaining stream-entry)

“I am” arises , we then grasp on it, and reflect “what am i”, then we conclude (“this i am”, “i am that” - referring to the 5 aggregrates) - This is the formulation of a view of self and defined it.

Even those he’s abandoned the lower 5 fetters, there is still the underlying tendency of “I am”, underlying desire of “I am”, even though he knows it isn’t in the 5 aggregrates.

“He should not delight in earth” - “because he must/should fully understand it” (pariññeyyam)

Disciple - Arahant (asekha)

One who has destroyed the Āsava (deep fundamental dilements: Sensual craving, craving for existance, and ignorance )

“He too, like the trainee, directly knows earth as earth, and having directly known earth as earth, he does not conceive earth, he does not conceive in earth, he does not conceive apart from earth, he does not conceive earth to be mine, and he does not delight in earth - why is that? Because he has fully understand it”

Doesn’t need to say “should not”, because its impossible for the arahant to misconceive the objects of experience (by way of craving, conceit and wrong views) - because he has fully understood it.

pariññātam - “it has been fully understand”. With that all ignorance is eliminated, and the defilements rooted in that, fall away too.

“free from lust, through the destruction of lust” - fully eliminated, as opposed to it disappearing in Jhana.

“free from delusion, through the destruction of delusion”. So he does not delight in the object.

The Buddha

“He too, like the trainee, directly knows earth as earth, and having directly known earth as earth, he does not conceive earth, he does not conceive in earth, he does not conceive apart from earth, he does not conceive earth to be mine, and he does not delight in earth - why is that? Because he has fully understood it to the end”

”- Because he has understood that delight (nandi) is the root of suffering , and that with being/existance as condition there is birth, and that for whatever has come to be there is aging and death.”

bhava - conditioned existance
